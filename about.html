About Me

My name is Ben Carpine, and I've followed Massachusetts high school basketball for as long as I can remember. Whether it was playing for my hometown of Rockland, following the MIAA tournament, or debating seedings with friends, hoops has always been a big part of how I experience this state.

I built MIAA Hoops because I wanted to bring the kind of data-driven analysis that college and professional basketball enjoy down to the high school level. Massachusetts has incredible talent and incredible programs. They deserve to be talked about with the same rigor. As an actuarial science student at the University of Connecticut (go Huskies!), working with data is a facet of my everyday life, and the opportunity to merge data analytics with basketball was one I couldn't turn down.

About This Project

MIAA Hoops started as a question: what if you could objectively rank every high school basketball team in Massachusetts? Not by record, but by actual performance, adjusted for who they played. The answer turned out to be a NET rating system built from the ground up using every regular season game played in the state.

The ratings are powered by an iterative margin-based algorithm that accounts for strength of schedule, point margins, and opponent quality — the same fundamental approach used by serious college basketball analytics platforms, applied here for the first time to Massachusetts high school hoops.

"A team's record tells you what happened. Their NET rating tells you why — and what it means."

The dataset covers every MIAA regular season game for both boys and girls basketball, sourced directly from official MIAA records. Every score, every team, every result — entered, verified, and run through the model. The result is the most comprehensive picture of Massachusetts high school basketball performance ever published in one place.

Why It Matters

High school sports coverage in Massachusetts — and across the country — is severely underserved compared to college and professional athletics. Local beat reporters do heroic work, but no one has had the tools to answer questions like: Is that team really as good as their record suggests? How does a South Shore program stack up against a program from Western Mass? Who has the toughest schedule in the state?

MIAA Hoops answers those questions. Every team profile shows not just results, but context — strength of schedule, predicted vs actual margins, and where a team truly stands among all 300-plus programs in the state. For coaches, fans, journalists, and anyone who loves the game, that context matters.

What's Next

This season's ratings are the foundation. Going forward, the plan is to update ratings in real time throughout the season, add tournament bracket projections before MIAA Selection Sunday, and build out year-over-year historical tracking so you can see how programs have evolved over time.

The goal is to make MIAA Hoops the definitive home for Massachusetts high school basketball analytics — a resource that players, coaches, and fans come back to every week of the season.
